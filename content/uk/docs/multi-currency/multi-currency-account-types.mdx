## Overview

Once you have been successfully onboarded to use our multi-currency service, you are issued a new **Institution Master Account** and **Institution Operating Account** to hold your multi-currency funds. Note that these are separate from your GBP accounts.

![Image showing your initial set of accounts](/assets/images/mccy-onboarding-accounts.svg "Initial set of multi-currency accounts")

Your <strong>Institution Master Account</strong> cannot be used like a normal account to send and receive payments. It is used to group all of your multi-currency accounts. Your <strong>Institution Operating Account</strong> is used to hold your own operations funds only.

Multi-currency accounts can be created using the [POST /mccy/v2/Accounts endpoint](./manage-multi-currency-accounts#create-a-new-account-real).

### Ways to organise your multi-currency accounts

Your **General Segregation** accounts and associated virtual accounts can be enabled for a single currency or multiple, depending on your needs. You should decide on the account structure that best fits your use case before creating accounts.

Examples you could choose are shown below:

![Image showing general segregation account options](/assets/images/mccy-account-types.svg "General segregation account options")

- **Example 1:** You can choose to have a General Segregation account that holds one currency (e.g. USD). This means when you create a virtual account under this real account, it will be enabled for that one currency.

- **Example 2:** You can choose to have a General Segregation account that holds specific currencies (e.g. EUR, SEK, and USD). This means when you create a virtual account under this real account, it will be enabled for those specific currencies.

- **Example 3:** You can choose to have a General Segregation account that holds all currencies. This means when you create a virtual account under this real account, it will be enabled for all currencies.

## Multi-currency account types

The purpose of an account is shown by its **Kind** which also determines the autogenerated prefix assigned to the account, for example, an account with the kind **General Segregated** is named **Gen Seg {Institution Registered Name}**.

<Callout colour="blue">
It is important to select the correct kind for an account when you create it, as this cannot be amended later.
</Callout>

![Image showing correct usage of usageType/kind](/assets/images/mccy-account-types2.svg "Correct usage of usageType/kind")

The following table outlines the various multi-currency account types and naming convention.

| Usage               | API value                           | Generated account name                                         |
|----------------------------|------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------|
| Your Funds                 | `YourFunds`                  | Institution Operating                                        |
| General Segregated         | `GeneralSegregated`          | Gen Seg *{Institution Registered Name}*  - *{Label}*         |
| Designated Segregated      | `DesignatedSegregated`       | Des Seg *{Institution Registered Name - Owner Name}*         |
| General Client             | `GeneralClient`              | Gen Client *{Institution Registered Name}* - *{Label}*       |
| Designated Client          | `DesignatedClient`           | Des Client *{Institution Registered Name - Owner Name}*      |
| General Safeguarded        | `GeneralSafeguarded`         | Gen Seg *{Institution Registered Name}* - *{Label}*          |
| Designated Safeguarded     | `DesignatedSafeguarded`      | Des Seg *{Institution Registered Name - Owner Name}*         |

#### Institution Operating Account (Your Funds)
The **Institution Operating Account**, also known as a **Your Funds** account, is used to hold your own operational funds only. This can include fees, interest earnings, and so on.

#### Segregated accounts
**Segregated** accounts exist to hold customer funds separate from your operational funds. For certain types of institutions, it is a regulatory requirement that customer funds are segregated from your institution's operational funds.

**General Segregated** accounts hold pooled funds for multiple customers and are used to create virtual accounts against.

**Designated Segregated** accounts hold funds for a single customer only.

#### Client accounts
**Client** accounts allow you to receive and hold money for (or on behalf of) your customers. These accounts separate your customers' CASS 7 investment funds from your own operating accounts. 

You’d likely opt for client accounts if you’re holding money on behalf of a customer, typically in connection with the Markets in Financial Instruments Directive (MiFID) or designated investment businesses.

**General Client** accounts hold pooled funds for multiple customers and are used to create virtual accounts against.

**Designated Client** accounts hold funds for a single customer only.

#### Safeguarded accounts
**Safeguarded** accounts ensure that your customer's money is held separately from your operating funds. For E-Money Institutions (EMIs) and Payment Institutions (PIs) having at least one safeguarded account in place is a Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) requirement. ​

​You can receive an FCA-compliant safeguarding letter from ClearBank proving your regulatory compliance in relation to the account. If you require safeguarded accounts, you must raise a Freshdesk ticket to request a safeguarding letter once you have created a General Segregation or Designated Segregation account. The account will only be safeguarded if you have received a safeguarding letter from ClearBank.

**General Safeguarded** accounts hold pooled funds for multiple customers and are used to create virtual accounts against.

**Designated Safeguarded** accounts hold funds for a single customer only.
