## Account types

When you onboard with ClearBank, the following accounts are created by default:

- **Institution Master Account** - this is not an account of its own; it is a superset of all your other accounts. 
- **Repair Suspense Account** - used to apply funds relating to your institution which cannot be applied to the intended creditor account. Funds held in this account should be re-routed to the correct account or sent back by you.
- **Operating Account** - holds your own operational funds. Your first Operating Account may be referred to as your **Primary Operating Account**.
- **General Segregated Account** - created as part of your onboarding journey.

![Image showing your initial set of accounts](/assets/images/accounts-eu-accounts-at-onboarding.svg "Initial set of accounts created during EU onboarding")

### Safeguarded accounts
Create the following accounts to hold safeguarded customer funds:
- Designated Segregation Account
- General Segregation Account

You can also create additional operational or safeguarding accounts as required.
Use virtual accounts within a general segregation account to manage multiple customers’ funds in a single real account.

<Callout colour="blue">
<p><b>Do not use segregation accounts to hold your own funds.</b></p>
</Callout>

### Account kind and prefix

When you create a real account use the `Kind` field to select:
- `YourFunds` 
- `DesignatedSegregated`
- `GeneralSegregated`

<Callout colour="blue">
<p>It is important to <b>select the correct usage for the account when you create it, as this cannot be amended later</b>. The account usage determines, for example, whether the account can be used for safeguarding funds.</p>
</Callout>

The `Kind` value determines the autogenerated prefix assigned to the account, for example, 'Gen Seg Acc Retl'. The prefixes for each account are shown in the figure below.
