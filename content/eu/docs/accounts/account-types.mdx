## Account types

There are several types of accounts that can be created. Which you should use will depend on your agreement with ClearBank, and the purpose of the account.

When you join ClearBank you are given the following accounts by default:

- Your **Mandated Minimum Balance (MMB)** account holds your institution's pre-agreed mandatory minimum balance. Funds here are subject to the terms of your institution's contract with ClearBank.
- Your **Repair Suspense Account** is an account used to apply funds relating to your institution which cannot be applied to the intended creditor account. Funds held in this account should be re-routed to the correct account or sent back by you.
- Your **Operational Account** holds your own operational funds. Your first Operational Account may be referred to as your **Primary Operational Account**.

Your <strong>Institution Master Account</strong> cannot be used like a normal account to send and receive payments. It is used to group all of your multi-currency accounts. Your <strong>Institution Operational Account</strong> is used to hold your own operations funds only.

![Image showing your initial set of accounts](/assets/images/accounts-eu-day-1.svg "Initial set of accounts")

New real accounts can be created using the [POST /mccy/v2/Accounts endpoint](./manage-real-accounts#create-a-new-account-real).

New virtual accounts can be created against existing **General Segregation Accounts** and **General Safeguarded Accounts** using the [POST /mccy/v1/VirtualAccounts endpoint](./manage-virtual-accounts#create-a-new-account-virtual).

### Real vs Virtual accounts

You can create multiple virtual accounts - each for a different customer - under a real account. This lets you keep several different customers' funds in a single real account.

**Real accounts are held with ClearBank.**  
The balances of real accounts are reconciled by ClearBank and can be checked on the ClearBank Portal or via our **[GET /v3/Accounts/\{accountId\}](/uk/docs/gbp-accounts/manage-accounts#get-information-for-an-account-real)** or **[GET /v3/Accounts](/uk/docs/gbp-accounts/manage-accounts#get-information-for-an-account-real)** endpoints.  

Real GBP accounts can be created using the **[POST /v3/Accounts endpoint](/uk/docs/gbp-accounts/manage-accounts#get-all-accounts-real)**.

<br />

**Virtual accounts are held on your platform, but their funds are kept in a real account held with ClearBank.**  
To create a virtual account with ClearBank, you must create it under one of your real general (also known as pooled) accounts. You can then use this account's virtual IBAN (vIBAN) to send and receive payments using funds from the associated real account.  
You can create virtual accounts using the **[POST /v2/Accounts/\{accountId\}/Virtual](/uk/docs/gbp-accounts/manage-accounts#create-accounts-virtual)** endpoint.  

You are responsible for managing and reconciling your virtual accounts' balances.

## Account names and kinds

The purpose of an account is shown by its **Kind**. 

The name of the account will be autogenerated using the following:
- A prefix of the account kind chosen 
- Your Institution Registered Name
- Label (for General account kinds)
- Owner Name (for Designated account kinds)

The **Label** can be chosen to help identify the types of customer funds pooled in the account whereas the **Owner Name** is used to identify the individual legal owner of the account.

The following table outlines the various account types and naming convention:

| Usage                      | API value                    | Generated account name                                       |
|----------------------------|------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------|
| Your Funds                 | `YourFunds`                  | *{Label}*                                                    |
| General Segregated         | `GeneralSegregated`          | Gen Seg *{Institution Registered Name}*  - *{Label}*         |
| Designated Segregated      | `DesignatedSegregated`       | Des Seg *{Institution Registered Name - Owner Name}*         |
| General Safeguarded        | `GeneralSafeguarded`         | Gen Seg *{Institution Registered Name}* - *{Label}*          |
| Designated Safeguarded     | `DesignatedSafeguarded`      | Des Seg *{Institution Registered Name - Owner Name}*         |


<Callout colour="blue">
It is important to select the correct kind for an account when you create it, as this cannot be amended later.
</Callout>

#### Institution Operational Account (Your Funds)
The **Institution Operational Account**, also known as a **Your Funds** account, is used to hold your own operational funds only. This can include fees, interest earnings, and so on.

#### Segregated accounts
**Segregated** accounts exist to hold customer funds separate from your operational funds. For certain types of institutions, it is a regulatory requirement that customer funds are segregated from your institution's operational funds.

**General Segregated** accounts hold pooled funds for multiple customers and are used to create virtual accounts against.

**Designated Segregated** accounts hold funds for a single customer only.

#### Safeguarded accounts
**Safeguarded** accounts ensure that your customer's money is held separately from your operating funds. For use by E-Money Institutions (EMIs), Payment Service Proviers (PSPs) and investment firms.

A safeguarding letter can be produced upon request, but it is not a requirement for safeguarded accounts. To request a safeguarding letter, raise a Freshdesk ticket.

**General Safeguarded** accounts hold pooled funds for multiple customers and are used to create virtual accounts against.

**Designated Safeguarded** accounts hold funds for a single customer only.
